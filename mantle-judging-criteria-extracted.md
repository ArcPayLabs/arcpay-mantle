## In General 50%
 | Judging process — two scorecards per judge / All judges are required to complete two scorecards for each project they review. / The first is the general scorecard, which applies universally across all tracks. It evaluates projects against Mantle's core judging dimensions: Technical, Ecosystem Fit, Business Potential, Innovation, and User Experience. / The second is a track-specific scorecard, customized by each track's title sponsor. This scorecard reflects the sponsor's focus areas and the unique criteria they have defined for their track. / Both scorecards must be submitted for a project's score to be counted.
 | Dimension | Weight | Key metrics | Description
 | Technical | 0.3 | Architecture design, security, code quality, feature completeness | Evaluates code robustness and technical complexity. Core functionality must run end-to-end on the Mantle network.
 | Ecosystem fit | 0.2 | Mantle stack integration, asset integration (DeFi / CeFi), ecosystem complementarity | Measures how effectively the project leverages Mantle's core features and its strategic contribution to the ecosystem.
 | Business potential | 0.2 | Product-market fit (PMF), tokenomics, viability, GTM strategy | Evaluates sustainable revenue potential, genuine user demand, and a credible post-hackathon go-to-market plan.
 | Innovation | 0.2 | Originality, differentiated advantage, technical or model breakthrough | Encourages novel ideas and approaches that address real industry pain points — not simple forks or clones.
 | User experience | 0.1 | UI/UX design, onboarding friction, AA / gasless integration | Focuses on product usability, especially efforts to lower the barrier for Web2 users entering Web3.
 | Grade | Score | Performance indicators
 | Excellent | 9 – 10 | Breakthrough technical innovation; seamless Mantle feature integration; production-ready with a clear and complete business logic loop.
 | Good | 7 – 8 | Stable, well-reasoned demo; clear points of innovation; provides evident value to the Mantle ecosystem.
 | Average | 5 – 6 | Core functions implemented and testnet-functional; represents a localized adaptation or incremental improvement of an established model.
 | Below avg | 3 – 4 | Incomplete features or major bugs; lacks meaningful Mantle integration; business logic is unclear or unconvincing.
 | Poor | 1 – 2 | Concept or slides only; direct plagiarism or fork with no original contribution; unable to demonstrate a working product.
